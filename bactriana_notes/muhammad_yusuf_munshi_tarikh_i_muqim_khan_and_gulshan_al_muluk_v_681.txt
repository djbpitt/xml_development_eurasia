Rukopisnyi Otdel Index #: В (V) 681
Catalog designation:  482. Tarikh.  Short history of the Bukharan emirate from 1022/1613 - 1246/1830. "... хронограммы и различные исторические заметки, в том числе имена 6 ханов, правивших в Балхе, Кундузе, Хисар, и т п."

*Note*: Because this work is not properly identified, I initially thought it might be an unknown work, but it is in fact Muhammad Yusuf Munshi's history, which has been translated: Moḥammad-Yusof Monši b. Kᵛāja Baqā Balḵi, Tāriḵ-e Moqim-ḵāni, tr. A. A. Semenov, Tashkent, 1956, in Russian.  

Moḥammad-Yusof Monši b. Kᵛāja Baqā Balḵi, Tāriḵ-e Moqim-ḵāni, tr. A. A. Semenov, Tashkent, 1956.

- Very popular work, but most copies were made in the 19th century.  "Можно полагать, что эта история по каким-то причинам стала известна гораздо позже, чем она была составлена" (5).
- Possible origin of the Petersburg copy?: "Русский посол Бузаре А.Ф. Нерги, посетивший Бухару в 1820-1821 гг., получил в подарок от эмира рукопись **Муким-ханской истории** и привез ее в Петербург" (6).
    - *Though this would be impossible since on p. 174 the date 1272 (1855) is listed.*
- Note that Semenov's translation only has the second book; both the six page first book and the Manghit section are not present.

Volume 3.

- The second book is in fact Gulshan al-Muluk (گلشن الملوک) by Muhammad Yakub, the twelfth son of Muhammad Danial, the second Manghit Amir (313).
- Miklukho-Maklai's entry for the first part of this manuscript (i.e. Tarikh-i Muqim-Khani) is entry 430; his entry for the second half (i.e. Gulshan al-Muluk) is entry 451.
- There are many different editions of the Gulshan al-Muluk; this one clips off the beginning, but contains the most valuable part that goes up to 1830 (316).
- The author mentions his name on p.147a (316).
- Miklukho-Maklai notes that the very end section of the book about Nasrullah starting on p174a  was probably written after the completion of the rest of it, very close to the events themselves (317).
- p. 175a lists rulers of Balkh, Kunduz, Tash-Kurgan, Hisar, and Ura-Tyube (317).

#### Perusal Index

- Fairly certain that pages 1-6 is just preamble, i.e. praise to God, Muhammad & co., etc.  These pages also make up "sub-book" 1.
- 12b: Tale of the rise of Chingiz Khan
    - 15: Chingiz and Khwarazmshah
    - 15b: Chingiz and Balkh; 16b: the plunder of Balkh and Tirmiz
- 19b: Maqala 1 on Shibani Khan
    - 27: A Shibani conquest of Balkh - during reign of عبدالله بهادر خان; he sents عبد المومن خان to Balkh.
    - 32: Abdallah Khan
- 33b: Maqala 2 on the Astrakhanids
    - 36b: Revenge in Kunduz
    - 39: Enthronement of a new khan ولی محمد خان  and sending his brother امامقلی سلطان  to Balkh
    - 46: Date - 1084 / 1673
    - 46b: Someone sent by Jahangir from Hindustan
    - 54: The battle with Hindustan for Balkh (I think).
    - 58b: Someone from Urgenj (judging by the name briefly takes the thrown of Mavrannahr and is killed quickly thereafter.
    - 67: Sending someone to Balkh
    - 68->: numerous enthronements in Balkh; 75: attack by a sultan of Balkh on Bukhara.
    - 80b: Aurangzeb of Hindustan sends on embassy in 1096/1684.
    - 88b: Something to do with the Kaisars of Rome i.e. Ottomans and Bukhara.
- 109: Maqala 3 on the enthronement of ابو المظفر سید محمد مقیم بهادر خان in Balkh
    - 115: year of 1115/1703
    - 126: Rahim Ataliq attacks Hisar
    - 130: something about Kunduz
    - 132: date listed at end of second book is 1225 (1810).
- 133b: Beginning of "sub-book" 3.
    - 134: Mentions Nadir's conquest of Bukhara in 1152/1739.
    - 136: Lists governors of all the different provinces
    - 150: Amir Mausum
    - 161: Haydar
    - 174: Date 1272 (1855) is mentioned.
