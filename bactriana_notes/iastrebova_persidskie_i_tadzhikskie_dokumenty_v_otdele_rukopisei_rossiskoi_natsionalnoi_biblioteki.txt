## O.M. Iastrebova, ed., Persidskie i Tadzhikskie dokumenty v Otdele Rukopisei Rossiskoi Natsional'noi Biblioteki (St. Petersburg: Izd. Rossiiskoi natsional'noi biblioteki, 1999).

As it turns out, the Russian National Library has a legitimate (though not vast) document collection, including, *inter alia*, a bunch of Samarkandi waqf documents from the 18th and 19th centuries (pp. 77-80).  Lots of stuff that *might* be interesting, relatively little that is spot-on.  Based on the introduction, the documents described in this book represent a fraction of the total.

Contents of this catalog: Настоящий каталог включает 248 описаний 246 оригинальных документов и двух сборникоа, содержащих 89 копий (13).  Organized based on whether they orginate from Iran (*section not scanned*) or Central Asia.

#### Central Asia Collection

Waqf documents

- 52. Kaufman 2. (p. 78).  A madrasa waqf in some village near Khazar.  Only of possible interest because of the early date - 1759.
- 54. Pers. dok. 58. (p. 78). Might be a good example of retaining wealth between generations via waqf. Samarkand 1835, parallel Russian translation. 10 pages.

Kupchi

- 60. Kaufman 5. (p. 81). A land sale in Kish 1777 only of note because it lists a lot of witnesses, many of whom are qazis, etc.  Of possible prosopgraphic use.
