General Notes

- chronological biographies (ordered by year of death) of ulama - as well as kings, notable governors - from the time of the Prophet through 1307 / 1889f from all over the world 
    - not just from Tatarstan.  Marjani includes biographies of Mughal, Central Asian, and especially Ottoman rulers.  Many - perhaps even a majority - of the ulama entries are Ottoman.  There seem to be almost as many ulama from the Maghrib as there are listed from Transoxiana.  Also worth noting that he covers all of the Madhabs, not just the Hanafis (particularly Shafi and to a lesser extent Maliki).  Even Wahabi
    - *Note*: There might be a bit of a "chicken or the egg" thing going on here; the preponderance of Ottoman scholars vis-a-vis Bukharan ones might have something to do with the lack of biographical dictionaries for Marjani to work from (he is going on word of mouth), not a lack of interest in Bukhara
    - Also, the geographical spread is not even over time; in the 18th century and earlier there is a preponderance of Ottoman scholars, but then in the 19th there are far more Tatar and Central Asian scholars, suggesting that Marjani was working from another work earlier on and then from oral sources once the time period got closer to his own time.
- Note that the "entry #" designation is written in pencil by the editor, not the original author.
- The dots for ی is never marked; I used ي when I was certain, but generally left it as ی, which could mean either ي or ا (but probably almost always the former).
