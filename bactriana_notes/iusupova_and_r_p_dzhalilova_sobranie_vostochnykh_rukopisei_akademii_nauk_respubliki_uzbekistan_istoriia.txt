## Iu. Iusupova and R. P. Dzhalilova, Sobranie vostochnykh rukopisei Akademii nauk Respubliki Uzbekistan. Istoriia (Tashkent: Izdatel'stvo **Fan** Akademii nauk Respubliki Uzbekistan, 1998).

#### Manghit History

تحفه الحانی Khanskii Podarok p. 176, #436, 5359/II

- Alternative title: Tarikh-i Rakhim-khani
- Author: Akhund Mulla Mukhammad Vafa b. Mukhammad Zakhir Kerminagi, Kadi Vafa (1096/1685-1183/1769).
- Scope: Covers events from 1134/1722-1182/1769, from the reign of Mukhammad Rakhim (1167/1753-1171/1758) to the beginning of the rule of Danial-bey (1171/1757-1199/1785).  Dushanbe copy (Kat. AN Tadzh., I, No. 105) covers an additional 14 years to 1196/1782.

فتوحات امیر معصوم و امیر حیدر Pobedy Amir Ma'suma (Shah-Murad) and Amir Khaidara p. 179, #498, 40/IV

- Author: Mirza Mukhammad Sadik "munshi" (d. 1235/1819).  Secretary for Shah-Murad (1199/1785-1215/1800) and Haidar (1215/1800-1242/1826).  Author was also a poet.
- Description: This is a poetical account of the conquests of Shah-Murad and Amir Haidar

#### Travel

سفرنامۀ قاضی هادی خواجه از بخارا به ایران p. 375, 897; 5255/XVIII; l. 150b-155a

- Bukharan mulla does the pilgrimage to Najaf and Karbala in 1743 / 1744. Describes meeting with Shah of Iran (i.e. Nadir Shah?) and religious disputes with Iranian mullas.

غرایب الخبر فی عجایب السفر p. 377, 900; 2106, 153 l.

- This is Vazih (of the poetical anthology)'s hajnamah.  He goes via Istanbul.
- In 1904 a lithograph was produced in Kagan (i.e. Bukhara).  Copied by hand numerous times as well.

در بیان راه حج p. 378, 904; 9379/III l. 154b-159b.

- Written by Kokandi Mulla Mirza 'Alim in 1888.  Goes through Bukhara and then Istanbul.  Chaghatai.

تحفه اهل بخارا p. 379, 907; 2142, 190 l. [[Sirāj al-Dīn Bukhārī, Tuḥfah-i Ahl-i Bukhārā (al-Biruni 2142)]]

- Written by a Bukharan merchant about his travels to Russia and back in 1911.
