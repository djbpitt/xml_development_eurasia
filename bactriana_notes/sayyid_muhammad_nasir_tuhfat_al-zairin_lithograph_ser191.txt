Note in Сокровищница восточных рукописей: Института востоковедения имени Абу Райхана Бируни Академии Наук Республики Узбекистан (UNESCO 2012, page 10): “A distinctive part of the [al-Biruni] collection features books published in India, Iran, Turkey and other countries, while the remaining pieces were published in Tashkent, Kokand, and other Central Asian cities. In this case, manuscript versions of some have either not survived or have not been found. Among them is a unique essay worth mentioning: ‘Tuhfat al-za’irin’ by Sayyid Muhammad Nasir al-Bukhari, son of the aforementioned Amir Muzaffar…” Mentioned also in Ron Sela’s “Historical Chronology of Central Asia” (i.e. 1906 version).
