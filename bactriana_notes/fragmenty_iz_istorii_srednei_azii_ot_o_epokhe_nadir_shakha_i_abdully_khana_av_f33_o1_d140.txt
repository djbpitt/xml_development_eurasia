## Fragmenty iz istorii Srednei Azii ot o epokhe Nadir-shakha i Abdully-khana F33 O1 D140

This delo consists of only a couple pages, apparently written on scrap paper.  The handwriting resembles that of Kun's mirza, Abdurrakhman (see [[index|Fond 33, A.L. Kun]]).  Also, the only other time I have seen the Persian plural on the Arabic plural of faqir (فقرایان) is in Abdurrakhman's [[draft Iskandarkul expedition diary|Dnevnik mirzy Abdurrakhmana, vedennyi vo vremia puteshestviia po Bukhare i ee okrestnostiam (AV F33 O1 D217)]], which makes it somewhat more likely that this information was recounted orally; also see his use of the word انداختن on p. 4, which he also uses to refer to establishing someone in a fortress in the diary.  The three pages have no relation to one another, so there are probably missing sections to these random notes.

Page 1 is about the conquests of Abdullahkhan (خاقان عبدلله خان ابن سکندر خان).

Page 2-3 are marred enough that I wasn't able to follow.

Page 4 on Nadir Shah (note the mirza's misspelling) is copied (almost) in its entirety:
ندیر شاه از قیزلباش آمده هماین (همه این) مملکت را گرفت بوده است. همان کافر (لکه) قلعه را انداخت بوده است 
(unable to even record a sentence due to smudging)
پنج سال در همان مملکت بوده است. بعد از پنج سال بازگشته رفت. در چشم های کوه ریخته رفت.  بعد از وی عبدلله خان (چیزی نا فهما) در مملکت صاحب شده.  جوی که نیکه عرق را بر اورد باز همین مملکت را آباد کرد
The last line does not make any sense, could be in Turkish: قرقجه تیسه کل در تیسه خشتسلک نیزه تیسه

I copied from this document because it is probably a kind of oral history collected by Kun when he passed through Bukhara. It is interesting how Nadir Shah came to be remembered when [[Hakim Khan's account|Muḥammad Ḥakīm Khān, Muntakhab al-Tavārīkh 1842: Nadir Shah]] is quite different.  Also, one can see how Abdurrakhman's status as a mirza really was something quite distinct from an alim; in that he often writes things the way they sound rather than how they are spelled (e.g. نیکه، ندیر).
