## Permission from Katta-Kurgan Nachal'nik for local to study in Bukharan madrasa (1910: i-126 / 1 / 1191)

//Would have to revisit to get through some paleographic / language issues, but the basic point is that the Russians were stepping in and bureaucratizing a centuries-old educational route.//

#### Russian Document (f. 1)
25 September 1910
Katta-Kurganskii U"ezdnyi Nachal'nik
No. 10722

- Individual in question: Mulla Berdimurad [Bey?] Ab al-Dhikr 
- Permission to go to Bukhara to study: ... согласно поданнаго им прошение разрешаю отправиться в город Бухару для получения там высшаго мусульманскаго образования...

#### Turki-language Pass (f. 3)

- Titled as آچیق خط, "open letter," *probably direct translation of удостоверение in the Russian version.*
- This is a blank printed form for traveling generally; the individual in question, his place of origin, and his destination are filled in with pen.
